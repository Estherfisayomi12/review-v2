Call for Submissions.

iWorder calls for submission to its first-ever writing workshop. opened to writers aged between 18 and 25. Slots are limited, as iWorder seeks a learning environment as intimate and closely-knitted as possible.

Workshop Details

  • The workshop will be held both online and in physical gatherings spontaneously over a span of two months. All physical meeting places will likewise be spontaneous but within the premises of Ile Ife, Osun state.
  • The workshop will be limited to nine African writers selected on the basis of their submitted samples or excerpts. Students admitted into the workshop, especially non-Ife residents will have to cover accommodation and feeding themselves. Although, the iWorder team will offer a stipend and accommodation for two students who can prove their need for them.
  • What do we want? We are not interested in the rigmarole structures of teaching. We seek writers that are particular about their crafts. We look forward to ones that are also very interested in having a writing family; writers are vines, we are forever reaching for images of ourselves.
  • The workshop will focus on the discussion and critique of the participantsâ€™ writing and the study of the craft through reading, active writing, analysis, editing, and consultation.

Submission Deadline

The submission period is from August 1st to August 21st. Successful applicants will be informed before September 2nd.

Eligibility

The workshop is open to: .

Writers based on the African continent (living in an African country) with at least one parent who is a citizen of an African country.

Writers who have not yet published a full-length novel with an established publisher (self-published authors may apply)
